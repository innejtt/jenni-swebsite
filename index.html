What

What is the Stock Market?

The stock market is a marketplace where investors can buy and sell shares of companies. It allows companies to raise capital by offering ownership stakes to the public.

What are the advantages and disadvantages?

         Advantages:

  • Potential for high returns on investment
  • Allows companies to raise funds for expansion
  • Provides liquidity to investors, meaning they can easily buy and sell shares

         Disadvantages:

  • Can be volatile and unpredictable
  • Potential for significant financial losses
  • Influenced by economic, political, and social factors

What are the risks and rewards?

         Risks:

  • Market volatility can lead to rapid loss of investment value
  • Poor company performance can decrease stock prices
  • External factors like economic downturns can affect the entire market

         Rewards:

  • Potential for capital gains if stock prices increase
  • Dividends paid by profitable companies
  • Long-term growth of investment if the market performs well over time

Why

Why do people invest in the Stock Market?

People use the stock market to invest money with the goal of earning a return. It allows individuals to grow their wealth, save for retirement, and reach financial goals.

Why is it important that we learn about it?

  • The stock market offers one the opportunity to have short- or long-term gains, however, not everyone is cut out for such investments
  • Nevertheless, understanding the stock market helps people make informed investment decisions, manage risks, and potentially achieve financial stability and growth. It also contributes to overall economic literacy

    • Who

    Who is involved?

             Investors and Traders
    • Institutional Investors (such as pension funds, mutual funds, insurance companies, hedge funds, etc.): Manage large amounts of money and often have a significant influence over the market since they are trading in large volumes
    • Brokers: Intermediaries who facilitate the buying and selling of stocks
    • Regulators: Bodies like the SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission) that oversee market activities to ensure fairness and transparency

    Who is allowed to/able to participate?

             In Canada:
    • Individuals, resident or non resident, who are legally adults (18 years old or the age of majority in their province or territory) are generally allowed to own shares in businesses/participate in the stock market, as long as they have the capacity to make financial decisions
    • Minors (individuals under the age of majority) may also invest in the stock market, but typically require a custodial account managed by a legal guardian or trustee

    How

    How does it work?

    The stock market operates through exchanges where stocks are listed. Investors place orders to buy or sell shares, which are matched by the exchange. Prices are determined by supply and demand.

    How does someone make/lose/borrow money through it?

             Borrow Money:
